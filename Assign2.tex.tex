\textbf{ [ICSE 12 2018]1(\romannumeral 2)}\\
If matrix $\vec{A} = \begin{pmatrix}
    5 & a \\ b & 0
\end{pmatrix}$ and $\vec{A}$ is symmetric matrix, then show that a = b .\\
\solution\\\
Given,
\begin{align}
    \vec{A} &= \myvec{5 & a \\ b & 0}
\end{align}
Also,
$\vec{A}$ is symmetric
\begin{equation}
    \implies\vec{A} = \vec{A^T} \label{2}
\end{equation}
As
\begin{align}
\vec{A} &= \myvec{5 & a \\ b & 0}, \vec{A^T} = \myvec{5 & b \\ a & 0 }
\end{align}
And,\\
We also know that two matrices are said to be equal if and only if  all the elements in the corresponding positions are equal.
So,
\begin{align}
    \vec{A} &= \vec{A^T} \implies A_{12} = A^T_{21}\\
    &\implies a=b
\end{align}
Where,\\
$A_{12} \implies$ The element in the second column of the first row of matrix $\vec{A}$ and \\
$A^T_{21} \implies$ The element in the first column of the second row of  matrix $\vec{A^T}$
\end{flushleft}
